Scenario 1 — Worst Case
Dollar Collapse & Debt Spiral
Likelihood
10–15%
The US loses reserve currency status. Debt becomes unserviceable. A crisis resembling Greece — but global in scale — forces a painful restructuring of the American economy.
What Has to Go Wrong
  • Iran war escalates, oil spikes to $150+/barrel
  • Dollar freefall — DXY drops below 80
  • US loses ability to roll over debt at affordable rates
  • BRICS accelerates dollar replacement
  • Buffett's cash can't stop a systemic collapse
Consequences
  • Hyperinflation — consumer prices up 20–30%+
  • Severe recession, unemployment 15%+
  • Interest payments consume 50%+ of federal budget
  • Social unrest, political instability
  • USD no longer world reserve currency

Scenario 2 — Bad Case
Stagflation & Managed Decline
Likelihood
30–35%
The US doesn't collapse, but enters a painful Japan-style "lost decade." High debt, stubborn inflation, stagnant growth. Living standards erode slowly. The dollar weakens but doesn't crash outright.
What Triggers This
  • Iran war lingers — oil stays elevated at $100–120
  • Tariffs raise consumer prices 5–8% permanently
  • Fed is caught between inflation and recession
  • Deficit spending continues unchecked
  • Venezuela/Russian oil doesn't fully offset Iran gap
Consequences
  • Inflation stays at 5–8% for years
  • Growth near zero — real wages keep falling
  • Dollar weakens another 15–20% over 5 years
  • US maintains reserve currency status — barely
  • Painful austerity eventually forced on Congress

Buffett's bet: Selective deployment into beaten-down US assets that still generate real earnings. This is the "muddle through" scenario — painful but survivable. Think 1970s America.

Scenario 3 — Moderate Case
Turbulent Reset & Stabilization
Likelihood
35–40%
Iran conflict resolves within 6–12 months. Oil prices normalize. The US dollar stabilizes at a lower-but-manageable level. Debt remains a slow-burning problem, but markets regain confidence.
What Has to Go Right
  • Iran neutralized — Strait of Hormuz reopens fully
  • Venezuela + Russia oil fills supply gap temporarily
  • Fed cuts rates as inflation cools to 3–4%
  • DOGE-style spending cuts make modest dent in deficit
  • Dollar stabilizes — DXY holds 90–95
Outcomes
  • GDP grows at 1.5–2.5% annually
  • Inflation cools but stays above 2% target
  • Stock market recovers — Buffett deploys some cash
  • Dollar remains reserve currency — no replacement
  • Debt/GDP stabilizes but doesn't meaningfully improve

Scenario 4 — Best Case
Energy Dominance & American Renewal
Likelihood
15–20%
The US corners global oil, Iran is neutralized quickly, Venezuela flows freely, and energy revenues + fiscal discipline combine to meaningfully address the debt spiral. Dollar strength returns. A genuine economic boom follows.
What Has to Go Right
  • Iran war ends fast — regime change / peace deal
  • US controls Venezuelan, Libyan, and Gulf oil flows
  • Cheap energy drives manufacturing back to the US
  • Deficit meaningfully reduced — markets reward US bonds
  • AI productivity boom accelerates GDP growth
Outcomes
  • GDP grows 3.5–4.5% — roaring economy
  • Inflation back to 2% target by 2028
  • Dollar strengthens — DXY back above 105
  • Debt/GDP begins declining for first time in decades
  • Buffett deploys $370B — calls it "the best buying opportunity of my lifetime"
